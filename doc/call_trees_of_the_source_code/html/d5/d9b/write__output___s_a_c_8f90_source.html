121 !######################## SAC Alphanumeric Seismos ############################
122 !
123 ! written by Markus Treml and Bernhard Schuberth, Dept. for Earth and Environ-
124 ! mental Sciences, Ludwig-Maximilians-University Munich, Germany
125 !
126 ! some words about SAC timing:
127 !==============================
128 !
129 !NPTS,DELTA,B,E:
130 ! These define the timing of the seismogram. E is calculated by sac. So, say
131 ! you have 100 NPTS, a DELTA of 0.5, and set B to 0, E should be 50.
132 ! Likewise setting B to -50 gives an E of 0. Cutting basically cuts out points
133 ! between the two times you designate based on these values.
134 !KZTIME and KZDATE:
135 ! Now things get funky. KZTIME defines the exact time that the trace begins
136 ! at. It has no affect on timing per se. You'll really notice its effect if
137 ! you read in two traces from different dates.
138 
139 ! Reference markers, (e.g. the o-marker) are not defined relative to this time,
140 ! but rather to the begin time (B) of the seismo, so if you adjust B, you also
141 ! need to adjust KZTIME to match. I would suggest experimenting with this until
142 ! you understand it. It is a little non-intuitive until you see it for yourself.
143 !
144 !-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
145 !
146 ! This file is essentially the alphanumeric equivalent of the SAC binary data
147 ! file. The header section is stored on the first 30 cards. This is followed
148 ! by one or two data sections. The data is in 5G15.7 format.
149 !----------------------------------------------------------------------
